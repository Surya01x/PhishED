What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cyberattack where criminals pretend to be trusted sources — like your bank, a delivery company, or a social media platform — to trick you into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data. Once they gain access, they can steal money, misuse your identity, or compromise your accounts.

These scams are effective because they target human emotions. Attackers often create a sense of urgency (“Your account will be locked soon!”) or excitement (“You’ve won a free gift!”) to pressure you into clicking links or sharing information without thinking twice.

Phishing can take many forms — from emails and text messages (smishing) to phone calls (vishing) and targeted attacks (spear phishing). Recognizing these tactics and staying alert is the first step toward keeping your digital life safe."
